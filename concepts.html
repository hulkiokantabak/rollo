Experimental Concepts

One open word, taken all the way to a finished, honest, self-contained thing.

Each piece in this series starts from a single word and is built end to end — interpreted, argued over by panels designed to disagree, fact-checked, audited, and published — without copying the one before it. The method stays; the shape changes every time.

I · KALÓN — beauty

Beauty is not one thing; it is a few recurring moves, and the last move belongs to the beholder. Five playable generative instruments and a finale composed from your own play.

hulkiokantabak.github.io/kalon

II · ROLLO — courage (you are here)

Courage after Rollo May: not the absence of fear but movement in spite of doubt and despair — played, not preached. A wordless marsh crossing where the only certain road goes backward, and nothing has color until you move through it. Its first version was a text page; the author ruled it a failure, and the lesson — text depicts, mechanics enact — became this game.

the marsh · about · colophon

III · LEXIS — curiosity

Curiosity as the loop by which a mind teaches itself a world it was not given the key to. A small language you read by writing it: you arrange glyphs, the field answers with the state they mean, and a wrong guess is the best move — it means the world is larger than your rule. Nothing is hidden and nothing is a puzzle to solve, only a grammar whose depth does not run out.

hulkiokantabak.github.io/lexis

IV · POTHOS — desire

Desire lived in one register: the distance between wanting and having. A garden of appointments — you sow a seed with a small hold, it tells you exactly when it will open (a day, a week, a season), and nothing you do can hurry it. Being there while it is open is witnessing; missing it is recorded plainly. The first piece in the series that happens mostly while the tab is closed, and the first that can be addressed to another person.
